INTRODUCTION

In an era marked by rapid industrialization, population growth, and resource exploitation, the concept of environmental sustainability has emerged as a guiding principle for responsible and ethical resource management. Environmental sustainability encompasses a holistic approach aimed at preserving the delicate balance of our planet's ecosystems, ensuring the well-being of current and future generations.

Environmental sustainability is a concept rooted in the responsible stewardship of natural resources to meet the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs. It emphasizes the intricate interconnectedness between ecological health, societal well-being, and economic viability. At its core, environmental sustainability seeks to strike a balance between human activities and the Earth's capacity to regenerate and support life. This holistic approach involves adopting practices that minimize ecological impact, reduce carbon footprints, and promote biodiversity conservation. With a focus on renewable energy, waste reduction, and conservation efforts, sustainable practices aim to address global challenges such as climate change, deforestation, and pollution.
